<p><p>The ultrafiltration membrane market is classified into two primary types: inorganic membranes and organic membranes. Inorganic membranes, typically made from materials like ceramics or metals, offer high thermal stability and chemical resistance, making them suitable for harsh environments and various industrial applications. Organic membranes, on the other hand, are made from polymeric materials and are favored for their flexibility, cost-effectiveness, and ease of fabrication. Each type caters to specific filtration needs, influencing industries like water treatment, pharmaceuticals, and food processing.</p></p>

<p><p>Vacuum pumps in the medical market are crucial for creating and maintaining vacuum conditions in various applications. Positive displacement pumps work by trapping a fixed volume of air and expelling it, making them ideal for applications needing consistent vacuum levels. Centrifugal pumps, on the other hand, rely on rotational energy to move air and create a vacuum, suitable for larger volumes and higher flow rates. Each type offers distinct advantages depending on the specific medical requirements, such as efficiency and reliability.</p></p>

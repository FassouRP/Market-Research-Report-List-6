<p><p>The lithium-ion battery market features two primary types of electrolytes: liquid and solid electrolytes. Liquid electrolytes are widely used due to their high ionic conductivity and established manufacturing processes, enabling efficient ion transport between electrodes. However, they pose safety risks like leakage and flammability. In contrast, solid electrolytes, although still emerging, offer improved safety and stability, higher energy density, and the potential for flexible designs. Their development could lead to next-generation batteries with enhanced performance and reduced hazards.</p></p>

<p><p>The peptide antibiotics sterile injectable drugs market encompasses two primary types: ribosomal and non-ribosomal synthesized peptides. Ribosomal peptides are synthesized by ribosomes using messenger RNA, leading to structured and predictable antibiotic properties. In contrast, non-ribosomal peptides are produced by enzymatic processes involving peptide synthetases, resulting in a diverse range of complex structures with unique mechanisms of action. Both types are essential in combatting antibiotic-resistant infections, each offering distinct advantages in formulation and therapeutic efficacy in clinical settings.</p></p>

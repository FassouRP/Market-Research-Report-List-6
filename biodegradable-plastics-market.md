<p><p>The biodegradable plastics market comprises several key types: PLA (Polylactic Acid), derived from renewable resources, is popular for packaging and disposable products. Starch blends combine starch with conventional plastics for enhanced biodegradability. PCL (Polycaprolactone) is a versatile, slow-degrading polymer used in various applications. Regenerated cellulose, made from cellulose, is used in films and fibers. PBS (Polybutylene Succinate) offers good processing characteristics, while PHA (Polyhydroxyalkanoates) is produced by microbial fermentation, serving diverse applications due to its biodegradability.</p></p>

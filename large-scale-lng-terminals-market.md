<p><p>Large-scale LNG terminals are categorized into different types based on their functions and locations. Liquefaction terminals convert natural gas into LNG for storage and transport, while regasification terminals re-convert LNG back into gas for distribution. These terminals can be classified as onshore, located on land, or offshore, situated at sea. Onshore terminals typically facilitate easier access and infrastructure, while offshore terminals offer advantages in space, environmental impact, and proximity to shipping routes, catering to various market needs.</p></p>

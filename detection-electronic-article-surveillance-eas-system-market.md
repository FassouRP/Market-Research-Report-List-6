<p><p>Detection Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) Systems are security solutions designed to prevent theft in retail environments. These systems utilize various technologies, such as radio frequency (RF), electromagnetic (EM), and acousto-magnetic (AM) to detect unauthorized removal of items from stores. EAS systems typically include tags affixed to merchandise, deactivation systems at exits, and detection antennas that trigger alarms when tags are not deactivated properly.</p><p>The Detection Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) System Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.8% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing retail theft, a growing emphasis on security in stores, and advancements in EAS technology. Notable trends include the integration of EAS with inventory management systems, enabling retailers to not only combat theft but also optimize stock levels. The rise of e-commerce has also influenced EAS systems, as retail establishments are looking for ways to incorporate robust security measures in physical locations. Additionally, the adoption of RFID technology is gaining traction within the EAS market, offering better tracking and inventory visibility. As retailers increasingly prioritize loss prevention, the demand for sophisticated EAS solutions is set to rise significantly.</p></p>

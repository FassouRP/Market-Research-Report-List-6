<p><p>The Carbon Fiber-Reinforced Carbon (C/C) market comprises two main manufacturing processes: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation. CVD involves depositing carbon-rich gases onto a substrate, facilitating the formation of a dense carbon matrix around the fibers. This process enhances mechanical strength and thermal stability. In contrast, Liquid Impregnation involves saturating carbon fibers with a liquid resin, followed by curing to create a composite material. Both methods are crucial in producing high-performance materials for aerospace, automotive, and industrial applications.</p></p>

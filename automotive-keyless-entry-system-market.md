<p><p>The automotive keyless entry system market is primarily divided into Passive Keyless Entry Systems (PKES) and Remote Keyless Entry Systems (RKES). PKES allows users to unlock and start their vehicles without physically using a key fob, relying on proximity sensors to detect the key fob's presence. In contrast, RKES requires users to press buttons on a key fob to unlock or start the vehicle. Both systems enhance convenience and security, contributing to their growing adoption in modern vehicles.</p></p>

<p><p>The consumer grade 3D printer market primarily consists of two key types: Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) and Stereolithography (SLA). FDM technology involves extruding molten thermoplastic through a nozzle, creating layers to build objects, widely favored for its affordability and ease of use. In contrast, SLA utilizes ultraviolet light to cure liquid resin layer by layer, offering higher precision and smooth finishes, making it popular for detailed projects. Both technologies cater to hobbyists and small businesses seeking accessible 3D printing solutions.</p></p>

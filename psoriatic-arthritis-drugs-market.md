<p><p>The psoriatic arthritis drugs market comprises several key types. TNF inhibitors target tumor necrosis factor, reducing inflammation and joint pain. PDE4 inhibitors work by blocking phosphodiesterase 4, which can alleviate symptoms by modulating immune responses. Interleukin blockers specifically inhibit interleukins involved in inflammatory processes, providing targeted relief. Other therapies may include corticosteroids, disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), or innovative treatments that don't fit neatly into the primary categories, expanding options for patient care and management of the condition.</p></p>

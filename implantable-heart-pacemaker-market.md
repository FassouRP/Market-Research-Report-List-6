<p><p>The implantable heart pacemaker market consists of two main types: temporary and permanent pacemakers. Temporary pacemakers are used for short-term management of heart rhythm issues, often in hospital settings, facilitating immediate stabilization after procedures or acute conditions. In contrast, permanent pacemakers are designed for long-term use, implanted to manage chronic heart rhythm disorders such as bradycardia. They provide ongoing electrical stimulation, ensuring the heart maintains an adequate rhythm and facilitating a better quality of life for patients.</p></p>

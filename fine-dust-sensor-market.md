<p><p>The fine dust sensor market can be categorized based on the particulate matter (PM) sizes they measure. Sensors that output PM10 detect particles with a diameter of 10 micrometers or less, capturing dust, pollen, and mold spores, crucial for assessing outdoor air quality. Conversely, sensors that output PM2.5 focus on finer particles, 2.5 micrometers or less, which are critical for health impacts, as they can penetrate deeper into the lungs. Both types are vital for monitoring air pollution effectively.</p></p>
